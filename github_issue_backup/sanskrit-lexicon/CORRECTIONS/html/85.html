funderburkjim commented on 2015-03-13 23:12:01

@sanskritisampada Regarding case 201. There are some special tricks to keep in mind when you edit issues in Github. One trick regards angle brackets. In 201, you entered:

ss : Previous word wrong entry <nimiktanEmiktika> Correct entry <nimittanEmittika>

BUT notice that when you look at the comment, the two words in angle brackets have vanished! There are various ways to emphasize words in a comment, which you could use instead of angle brackets. For instance you could italicize the words by putting an asterisk before and after the word, or you could make the words bold by putting two asterisks before and after the word. For instance, by typing your comment as:

ss : Previous word wrong entry **nimiktanEmiktika** Correct entry **nimittanEmittika**

It would display as

ss : Previous word wrong entry nimiktanEmiktika Correct entry nimittanEmittika

As an exercise to learn this, why don't you change your 201 comment to show the words in italics, and look at the other places where you made a comment and change the angle-bracketed words to either italic or bold, whichever you prefer.

By the way, for the full story on how what you type in a comment is displayed, click on the Markdown supported link above.
