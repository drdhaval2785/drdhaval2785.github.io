funderburkjim commented on 2014-10-19 20:46:00

I concur with this observation.
Here is a bit of history regarding the MW supplement.

These additions/corrections records were originally in a separately digitized file, MONIER.ADD from Thomas. This file is about 400k.

They were 'tacked on the end' (With Lnumbers 300000-399999) of monier.xml in 2010. This also involved converting the form from that of MONIER.ADD to that of monier.xml. I probably did this.

in 2012, work by Peter Scharf, Pawan Goyal, and Sharon Ben-Dor (a former student of Peter) permitted the integration of the supplement into the body of monier.xml. Essentially, this involved identifying <Hx> codes and appropriate new Lnumbers for the supplement records. The coding was done in such a way that it is possible to identify records that originated in the supplement. As I recall, this is by means of an attribute of the Lnumber element.

Thus, it is likely possible to extract, in supplement order, the records of mw.xml that originated from the supplement. Then, someone could read these records in relation to the scans.
I count 6602 records in mw.xml matching '<L ' ; 6397 of these are of form <L supL="X"> and the rest are <L revL="X"> indicating supplements (additions) and revisions, respectively. There about 25.5 scan pages involved.

funderburkjim commented on 2014-11-19 02:56:01

There are two forms of L that indicate a records from the supplement:

<L supL="X">Y</L>   supplement (i.e., addition, new word)
<L revL="X">Y</L>    revision  (change some detail of word already in body)

(Incidentally, 'X' is the FORMER L-number; before the supplement records were woven into
 the body of mw.xml, they all appeared 'at the end'; the at-the-end L-number is X. Currently, 
X is not of interest, but it was of interest while the interweaving process was active.
However, if you wanted to make a list in the order of the print edition supplement, then you 
would sort on X.)

By filtering mw.xml using regex "<L " (L with space), you get exactly the set of MW records in the supplement.
