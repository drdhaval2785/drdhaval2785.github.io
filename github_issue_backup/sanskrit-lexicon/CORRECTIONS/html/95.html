@drdhaval2785
The extraction of INM records from AllvsMW.txt is here

An attempt was made to generate fuzzy suggestions.

This inm-fuzzyalpha.txt file has 3 lines for each of the cases shown above:

  • the previous headword (according to dictionary ordering - Note INM is not alphabetical in general). Sometimes this will help you find the problem word on the scan page. Sometimes this word may also be spelled wrong!
  • The word of the case, with suggestions. This line can have one of 4 similar forms:
    • case# word -> s1,...,sm [m suggestions, each alphabetically between words of lines 1 and 3]
    • case# word -> (t1,...,tn) [n suggestions, none alphabetically between words of lines 1 and 3]
    • case# word -> s1,...,sm (t1,...tn) A mixture of the other two cases.
    • case# word -> () No suggestions!
  • the next headword - sometimes this also may be spelled wrong
  • Your mileage may vary with regard to the usefulness of the suggestions. Sometimes they are quite helpful, and sometimes they are not very helpful.

Here is the workflow I have used:

  • Step 0: have local inm-fuzzyalpha.txt open in editor, and have this github issue page open
  • Step 1: Iterate through the cases:
    • use links on github issue to find the scan page, and locate the word on the scan page
    • if main word is in error, show its correction in fuzzyalpha.txt as case# word -> correction : [any other text] Note the colon character ends the useful part of the line, though other stuff can appear afterwords
    • if main word is correct, show it in fuzzyalpha.txt as case# word -> NO CHANGE : [any other text]
    • Similarly correct previous and next words if needed
    • that ends work for this case in step 1 iteration
  • Step 2: Once fuzzyalpha is finished, go back and copy/paste answers into comments in this Github page.
  • Step 3: Process the modified fuzzyalpha. I have a program that does most of the work of generating corrections based on the modified fuzzyalpha file. You could upload your modified fuzzyalpha file to, say, inm-fuzzyalpha-completed.txt, and I'll use it to install the corrections at Cologne.

Let me know if the approach I've set up for you with INM is good. If so, I'll do similar things for the other dictionaries that remain to be examined via faultfinder.

funderburkjim commented on 2015-04-14 19:12:44

Re is the colon necessary - Short answer is NO - should be ok to omit colon, as long as nothing else is on the line. A correction line in fuzzyalpha is recognized as one containing '->'.
Suppose nnn hw -> newhw : some comment or other is such a line of fuzzyalpha. It is parsed first by splitting the line by colon character, and taking the first piece (e.g. the first piece is nnn hw -> newhw and then further parsing etc done from there. If there is no colon, this logic still works. So, that's a longer explanation of why colon is not necessary.
