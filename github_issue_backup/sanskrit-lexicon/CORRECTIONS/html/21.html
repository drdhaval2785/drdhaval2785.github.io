Key2 is exact reproduction of dictionary.

Both have their usefulness. Let's continue with your example. Here is the MW page of acCAi

Its key1 is 'acCAi'. Its key2 is 'a/cCA-i'.

Method:

  1. Click on the hyperlink
  2. Check the digital data of that word online

funderburkjim commented on 2014-11-12 03:10:38

re: 12 matteBAsya -> matteBaBAsya ?? I did not change. Here's the reasoning

  1. the text looks like ºbhâsya (?)and comes after -vi-krIqita (slp1). matte-BAsya is alphabetically after matte-Ba-vi-krIqita, but matte-Ba-BAsya would be out of order.
  2. The â usually indicates a long 'A' formed by vowel sandhi; in this case of bha and maybe 'asya' (I'm not sure of 'asya', but MW has a (?) so maybe he's not sure of this either).

  3. The º usually means that this is not a compound of the implied 'parent' (matte-Ba) if it were a compound of matte-Ba, then we would expect to text to show -bhâsya.

    Points 1 and 3 seem solid evidence in favor of no change.
