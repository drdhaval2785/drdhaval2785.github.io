  "body": "This issue is devoted to spelling corrections in the IEG dictionary (Indian Epigraphical Glossary).\r\n\r\nThere are 427 cases, comprising headwords where:\r\n* faultfinder found spelling differences from headwords of MW, and\r\n* spelling difference was not of the 'rxx' variety, and\r\n* headword appears in sanhw1.txt list only for IEG dictionary,\r\n\r\nSince 427 cases is a rather large number to manually check, a further programmatic examination was made.\r\nThere are many headwords in IET from languages other than Sanskrit.  A random examination of cases\r\nsuggested that many of the headwords are likely from the Tamil language.  Based on this, a program\r\nseparated the 427 cases into two groups:\r\n* 174 headwords whose entry in IEG contain the word 'tamil'\r\n* 253 other headwords.\r\n\r\nThe standard two fuzzy alpha files (.txt and .html) were prepared for each of these two subgroups, as\r\nwell as for the whole group.\r\n\r\n<table>\r\n<tr><th>group</th> <th>number</th> <th>txt file</th> <th>html file</th></tr>\r\n<tr style='background-color:green'><td>non-Tamil</td>  <td>253</td>  <td><a href=\"https://github.com/sanskrit-lexicon/CORRECTIONS/blob/master/dictionaries/IEG/faultfinder/ieg-fuzzyalpha_not_tamil.txt\">txt</a></td>  <td><a href=\"http://funderburkjim.github.io/fuzzywork/ieg-fuzzyalpha_not_tamil.html\">html</a></td></tr>\r\n\r\n<tr><td>Tamil</td>  <td>174</td>  <td><a href=\"https://github.com/sanskrit-lexicon/CORRECTIONS/blob/master/dictionaries/IEG/faultfinder/ieg-fuzzyalpha_tamil.txt\">txt</a></td>  <td><a href=\"http://funderburkjim.github.io/fuzzywork/ieg-fuzzyalpha_tamil.html\">html</a></td></tr>\r\n\r\n<tr><td>All</td>  <td>427</td>  <td><a href=\"https://github.com/sanskrit-lexicon/CORRECTIONS/blob/master/dictionaries/IEG/faultfinder/ieg-fuzzyalpha.txt\">txt</a></td>  <td><a href=\"http://funderburkjim.github.io/fuzzywork/ieg-fuzzyalpha.html\">html</a></td></tr>\r\n\r\n\r\n</table>\r\n\r\nIt is expected that only the first group (non-Tamil) should require examination, and that the Tamil group\r\nshould be considered as NO CHANGE without further examination.\r\n\r\nIt is also certain that some of the non-Tamil group contains words in other languages (English, Greek, Telugu, Persian, maybe others) and will also be marked as NO CHANGE. \r\n\r\nThe non-Tamil group is ready for someone to adopt - please mention in a comment if you are adopting, so others will\r\nnot duplicate your work.\r\n",
